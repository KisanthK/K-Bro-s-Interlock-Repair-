How We Work

The K Bros Interlock Repair Process

There's more to fixing interlock than pulling up a few stones and dropping them back in. Here's what actually happens on a K Bros job in Newmarket — and why every step matters.

  1. 1

    Site Assessment & Diagnosis

    Before we touch anything, we assess the full picture: base depth, drainage patterns, soil conditions and the true extent of damage. Most interlock problems trace back to base failures or drainage issues. Treating just what you can see is why so many repairs don't last.

    Why it matters: A sunken stone is usually a sign of a compromised base. Fix the stone without fixing the cause and you'll have the same problem within a year.
  2. 2

    Stone Removal & Area Preparation

    Affected stones are carefully removed and set aside where possible for reinstallation. The bedding layer is cleared down to the base. Pulling stones by hand or with the wrong tools chips and cracks the surrounding stones that are otherwise fine.

    Why it matters: Sloppy removal expands the scope of the repair. We keep the damage contained to what actually needs attention.
  3. 3

    Base Correction & Compaction

    The sub-base is regraded, compacted and rebuilt with fresh angular limestone where needed. Getting slope right for drainage while maintaining uniform depth takes experience and the right equipment. This is the step most homeowners skip entirely.

    Why it matters: Without proper compaction the base will shift again. Hand tamping doesn't get close to what a plate compactor achieves.
  4. 4

    Stone Replacement & Resurfacing

    Stones that are damaged beyond reuse get replaced with matched material from our supplier network. Getting colour, texture and dimensions right so the repair blends in takes product knowledge and supplier relationships most homeowners don't have access to.

    Why it matters: A visibly patched repair tells the story of the problem. We source material that matches so the finished job looks like it was never touched.
  1. 5

    Precision Re-laying & Alignment

    Stones go back on fresh bedding sand aligned to the existing pattern. We work to under 3mm level tolerance across the repaired area. Every stone gets physically checked for stability before we move on.

    Why it matters: Even a 5mm height difference creates a trip hazard and accelerates wear on the surrounding stones from traffic and freeze-thaw movement.
  2. 6

    Joint Filling & Mortar Repair

    Professional-grade polymeric sand is swept into the joints and activated with water. This locks the stones together and stops weed growth permanently. On stone steps, mortar joints get repointed with a matching compound.

    Why it matters: Regular sand washes out in one season. Polymeric sand creates a firm joint that holds up to traffic, water and insects.
  3. 7

    Final Inspection & Warranty

    A senior team member walks the job before we pack up: level, pattern consistency, joint fill, drainage slope. Once everything checks out, your 3-year warranty is activated and documented. We don't leave until the job is right.

    Why it matters: Our warranty only works because we stand behind it. With 240 Homestars reviews, Newmarket homeowners know what they're getting with K Bros.

Why DIY Interlock Repair Usually Makes Things Worse

We hear from Newmarket homeowners who tried to fix their interlock themselves and called us to redo the whole thing 12–18 months later. The issues come up the same way every time:

  • No plate compactor means the base never gets properly set — and Newmarket winters will expose that fast
  • The underlying drainage or base failure gets missed because it isn't visible from the surface
  • Incorrect base depth and grading leads to the same settling problem coming back after the first frost
  • Consumer polymeric sand isn't formulated for vehicle traffic and needs specific moisture activation to cure properly
  • Matching aged interlock stone without supplier access is nearly impossible

A professional repair done right the first time costs less than two attempts at doing it yourself. The K Bros 3-year warranty makes that comparison straightforward.
